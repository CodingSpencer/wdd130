Fun Facts About Captain America

  1. In the film, Avengers Endgame, Chris Evans had the most screentime as Captain America.
  2. It's Been a Long, Long Time by Kitty Kallen was played in two MCU films: Captain America: The Winter Soldier and Avenger Endgame.
  3. In Captain America: Civil War, Bucky admits that he remembers every action as the Winter Soldier to Ironman.
  4. Chris Evans originally said no to the role of Steve Rogers.
  5. Many fans believe the Captain America was able to hold Mjolnir in Avengers Age of Ultron, but did not to spare Thor's pride.
  6. In Avengers Infinity War, Steve Rogers becomes the Captain, a new rogue hero that has deserted the US Government.
  7. Steve Rogers shield is made of vibranium, a virtually indestructible metal, until it was broken by Thanos' atomantium weapon.
  8. Steve was the only avenger who didn't return home when Ironman asks "Isn't that the mission? So we can end the fight? So we get to go home?"
  9. Chris Evans had been in other movies with Scarlett Johansen before Marvel.
  10. Captain America's full name is Steven Grant Rogers.

MCU Films Including Chris Evans

Captain America: The First Avenger

Steve Rogers, a skinny kid from Brooklyn, wants nothing more than to serve his country. He is recruited for a super soldier experiment after being noticed by a german scientist. The Red Skull leads a rogue Nazi science division that threatens to conquer the world named Hydra. As Steve Rogers becomes the first super soldier will he be able to save the world from Red Skull?

Captain America: The Winter Soldier

Steve Rogers tries to find his place in a new world after waking up 70 years following WW2. He joins SHIELD to help protect and serve once again. However, secrets seem to dwell behind every corner and he doesn't know who to trust. And as a ghost reimurges from his past, Captain America has to choose whether to save an old friend or let an old foe rise to power.

Captain America: Civil War

The Avengers survived a major attack by Ultron, but had a high casualty count in the country Sokovia. The world governments on pushing to have regulations placed on the Avengers to better maintain control. When Tony Stark and Steve Rogers disagree on this, the remaining members of the team are forced to pick sides. And when the Winter Soldier becomes involved, things begin to look as though they may not be as they seem.

Avengers 1-4

Even when Steve Rogers first became Captain America he always worked in a team. So obviously, when Nick J. Fury began to recruit earth's mightiest heroes onto a team Rogers made the list. All the different heroes are not the most compatible with each other, but if they can't get past their differences Earth might be destroyed. Can Rogers lead the Avengers to victory or will the Earth be lost due to their failure?
